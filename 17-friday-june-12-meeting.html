Ad-Hoc Vs GenomegaMap

Why no overlap is not automatically bad

I showed that the ad-hoc mutation-count ranking and the GenomegaMap confidence-interval hits did not overlap much in the small comparison I had ready. Dr. Ioerger's point was that the ad-hoc method is intentionally rough. It counts observed synonymous and non-synonymous changes, while GenomegaMap is estimating a posterior distribution for a model parameter.

QuestionAd-hoc countsGenomegaMap
What is summarized? Observed S and NS mutation counts per gene/cohort. Posterior samples for parameters such as omega0, kappa, and theta.
What can inflate it? Zero denominators, small counts, lineage markers, and resistance mutations. Model assumptions, trace quality, and how much signal exists in the aligned sequences.
How should I use it? As a quick independent screen and sanity check. As the main Bayesian selection summary for the current workflow.
How to avoid overclaiming
A large NS/S score is a lead, not an answer. For every candidate, I should show the raw counts, the exact amino-acid changes, whether known resistance sites are involved, and whether the GenomegaMap posterior supports the same story.

MCMC Histograms

How to make the posterior visible

For a candidate gene, the table mean and 95% interval are useful, but a histogram of the omega0 samples makes the posterior shape much easier to explain. In the meeting, we looked at a candidate whose distribution was shifted above 1, with a lower interval bound just above 1.

PlotWhat it tells me
Trace plot Whether the sampler is wandering around a stable region instead of drifting or failing.
Histogram Where most posterior mass is, how wide the uncertainty is, and whether 1 is inside the high-density region.
Credible interval Whether the lower bound is above 1 for positive selection, or whether the interval overlaps neutral evolution.
